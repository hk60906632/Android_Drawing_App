General feedback

I gave marks out of 20, 40, 40 for development, technical and creative issues.

The development mark is for showing that you've learned something from the unit. So, it is for having multiple classes, showing a sense of design, and getting a whole program together and working, possibly using an IDE or other tools.

The technical mark is for exploring graphics in some direction or other, and showing off some sophistication. This might be in animation, or user interaction, or special library features, or integrating graphics with some other logic.

The creativity mark is for interesting ideas, or twists on old ideas, or technical exploration of possibilities, or polish, or artistic flair, or anything else that impressed me.

For many of you, your model is closely associated with graphics, making testing difficult. That is OK for an assignment of this size, and I wasn't picky about it in the marking. But it is important to remember that in larger projects, just because you have a GUI, that's no excuse for ignoring best practices. You need small or smallish classes, with tiny methods, total robustness, low dependencies, and plenty of automatic testing. Graphics libraries tend to force your design in a particular direction, but it is up to you to decide who is in control. So make this a priority for the next project you do that involves graphics.

Take Tetris for example, which several of you implemented. Maybe you got the logic from a tutorial. If so, you probably need more practice in developing logic without a tutorial. If not, the logic is sufficiently complex to need independent, automatic testing. If I was implementing Tetris, my graphical view component would draw a grid of coloured squares, nothing else. It would know nothing about pieces or their shapes or their movements or the progress of the game. After each time step, the view would ask the model what colour to draw each square. That's all. The model would deal with all the logic, using a grid of characters or other symbols, and have no dependency whatsoever on graphics library classes. In fact, to avoid being influenced by the graphics library, I would write the model first, with automatic testing, and with text printout for interactive testing, before adding a GUI.
